Development Lifecycle

A step-by-step journey from data ingestion to application deployment.

Phase 1

Data Exploration (EDA)

Deep dive into the raw dataset to understand structure and identify quality issues.

  • Ingestion: Loaded 400 records of sleep/lifestyle metrics.
  • Cleaning: Split "126/83" BP strings into numerical features.
  • Insight: Found -0.81 correlation between Stress & Sleep Quality.
  • Strategy: Identified class imbalance needing SMOTE.
Phase 2

Advanced Feature Engineering

Enriched data with derived metrics to capture complex health signals.

  • Sleep_Efficiency: Calculated normalized sleep performance metric.
  • Health_Risk_Score: Engineered weighted composite risk score.
  • Encoding: Applied One-Hot (Nominal) & Ordinal encoding strategies.
  • Zero-Vector Fix: Solved feature mismatch for single-row prediction scenarios.
Phase 3

ML Model Development

Training and optimizing the predictive "brain" of the system.

  • Regression: XGBoost selected for Sleep Quality (RÂ² â‰ˆ 0.98).
  • Classification: Random Forest selected for Disorders (F1 â‰ˆ 0.93).
  • Optimization: Utilized SMOTE to handle minority classes effectively.
  • Serialization: Models saved as .pkl files for deployment.
Phase 4

Strategic Analytics (Power BI)

Designing the high-level view for stakeholders and population management.

  • Executive View: KPIs for avg quality (6.13) & disorder rate.
  • Risk Analysis: Advanced scatter plots for Stress vs. Risk.
  • DAX Logic: Dynamic correlation & risk segmentation measures.
  • Outcome: Proved poor sleep is a symptom of lifestyle stress.
Phase 5

Intelligent App (Streamlit)

Developing the clinical-grade, user-facing diagnostic tool.

  • Smart Logic: Context-aware recommendation engine.
  • Robust Storage: Hybrid Session+CSV persistence system to fix cloud data loss.
  • Deployment Fixes: Solved "File Not Found" errors using absolute pathing and auto-initialization of data files.
  • UI/UX: Premium Glassmorphism Dark Mode interface.

Visual Intelligence Gallery

Population Health Overview

The "30-second view" for stakeholders. Features high-level KPIs (Avg Sleep Quality: 6.13, Disorder Rate: 27.5%) and distribution donuts. Validates the hypothesis that sleep disorders are prevalent in over a quarter of the population.

Occupational Risk Profiling

Breaks down metrics by Age, Gender, and Occupation. Highlights critical disparities, identifying "Sales Representatives" and "Nurses" as high-stress groups with significantly lower sleep quality scores compared to "Engineers".

Advanced scatter plots correlating `Stress Level` with `Health_Risk_Score`. This view visually proves the -0.81 correlation, demonstrating that poor sleep is a symptom of lifestyle stress rather than an isolated condition.
